Recent Reads (May)

Art Spiegelman - Maus
8.5/10

This was very good, and I totally respect it, but I didn't enjoy it quite as much as I was expecting. It's hard to put my finger on exactly why.

Ray Bradbury - Fahrenheit 451
3/10

Cheating here; I read this in Grade 9. Re-evaluating it after discovering this wild quote from the author:

"It works even better because we have political correctness now. Political correctness is the real enemy these days. The black groups want to control our thinking and you can’t say certain things. The homosexual groups don’t want you to criticize them. It’s thought control and freedom of speech control."

- Ray Bradbury, 1994, when asked if the book is still relevant today


Originally he said the book was about the dangers of censorship. Then a few years later he said it was actually about the dangers of advancing technology, and then after that he said it's about... the dangers of being told not to say slurs? What? Anyway, the book is well-written I guess, but it's as deep as a puddle. The more I think about it, the dumber it all sounds and the more I hate it. I couldn't imagine how anyone could put it anywhere near something like Nineteen Eighty-Four or even Brave New World.

Ernest Hemingway - The Old Man and the Sea
8/10

I feel like you could interpret this in a million different ways or just see it as a simple story. I don't know what Hemingway was intending here but I found it entertaining regardless. I might be being too generous with this rating for what's really here.
